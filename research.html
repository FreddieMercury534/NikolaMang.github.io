Past and Current Research Projects

MAGIC Telescopes

In April 2021 I worked a shift at the MAGIC telescopes in on the island of La Palma, Spain. MAGIC is a system of two Cherenkov telescopes, MAGIC I and MAGIC II, that are used primarily to detect Gamma rays, although they can also be used to search for particles like muons and neutrinos. As an operator, my tasks included taking data, providing hardware and software maintenance as well as start-up and shut-down of the telescopes.

The image shows one of said telescopes.

AutoMAGIC

Following my two shifts at the telescopes I was involved in work on the analysis pipeline of the MAGIC telescopes. The goal of the project is to almost completely automatise the analysis of data taken with the MAGIC telescopes. I was mostly involved in working with the python extension Gammapy.

Study of phase transistions

In a recent internship I studied the theoretical descriptions of phase transistions using the Clausius-Claperyon equation. My tasks consisted mainly of numerical integration using Fortran.
